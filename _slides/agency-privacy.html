--- layout: default path: agency-privacy title: "A-130: Agency Privacy Programs - General Requirements" --- ## Agency Privacy Programs ### General Requirements --- ## Establish and Maintain a Comprehensive Privacy Program * Agencies shall establish and maintain a comprehensive privacy program that ensures compliance with applicable privacy requirements, develops and evaluates privacy policy, and manages privacy risks. * Citation: Main Body § 5(f)(1)(a); Appendix I §§ 3(b), 3(f), 4(e). -- ### See Also * [OMB Circular A-123](https://www.whitehouse.gov/sites/whitehouse.gov/files/omb/memoranda/2016/m-16-17.pdf), Management’s Responsibility for Enterprise Risk Management and Internal Control at § 7(A) (July 15, 2016). --- ## Ensure Compliance with Privacy Requirements and Manage Privacy Risks * Agencies shall ensure compliance with all applicable statutory, regulatory, and policy requirements and use privacy impact assessments and other tools to manage privacy risks. Agencies shall cost-effectively manage privacy risks and reduce such risks to an acceptable level. ＊ Citation: Main Body §§ 4(g), 5(e)(1)(d), 5(f)(1)(a); Appendix I § 3(a), 3(b)(4), 3(f), 3(g). -- ### See Also * [OMB Circular A-123](https://www.whitehouse.gov/sites/whitehouse.gov/files/omb/memoranda/2016/m-16-17.pdf), _Management’s Responsibility for Enterprise Risk Management and Internal Control_ (July 15, 2016); NIST Special Publication 800-37, Risk Management Framework for Information Systems and Organizations: A System Lifecycle Approach for Security and Privacy; NIST Special Publication SP 800-39, Managing Information Security Risk: Organization, Mission, and Information System View. --- ## Monitor Federal Law, Regulation, and Policy for Changes * Agencies shall monitor Federal law, regulation, and policy for changes that affect privacy. * Citation: Main Body § 5(f)(1)(c) --- ## Develop and Maintain a Privacy Program Plan * Agencies shall develop and maintain a privacy program plan that provides an overview of the agency’s privacy program, including a description of the structure of the privacy program, the resources dedicated to the privacy program, the role of the SAOP and other privacy officials and staff, the strategic goals and objectives of the privacy program, the program management controls and common controls in place or planned for meeting applicable privacy requirements and managing privacy risks, and any other information determined necessary by the agency’s privacy program. * Citation: Appendix I § 4(c)(2), 4(e)(1). --- ## Designate a Senior Agency Official for Privacy * Agencies shall designate a SAOP who has agency-wide responsibility and accountability for developing, implementing, and maintaining an agency-wide privacy program to ensure compliance with all applicable statutes, regulations, and policies regarding the creation, collection, use, processing, storage, maintenance, dissemination, disclosure, and disposal of PII by programs and information systems, developing and evaluating privacy policy, and managing privacy risks at the agency. * Citation: Main Body § (5)(f)(1)(b); Appendix I § 4(e); Appendix II § 4. -- ### See also * [Executive Order 13719](https://www.gpo.gov/fdsys/pkg/FR-2016-02-12/pdf/2016-03141.pdf), Establishment of the Federal Privacy Council at § 2 (Feb. 12, 2016); * [OMB Memorandum M-16-24](https://www.whitehouse.gov/sites/whitehouse.gov/files/omb/memoranda/2016/m_16_24_0.pdf), Role and Designation of Senior Agency Officials for Privacy (Sept. 16, 2016). --- ## Ensure Coordination Between Privacy and Other Programs * Agencies shall ensure that the SAOP and the agency’s privacy personnel closely coordinate with the agency CIO, senior agency information security officer, and other agency offices and officials, as appropriate. * Citation: Main Body §§ 4(h), 5(f)(1)(k); Appendix I §§ 3(b)(11), 4(e)(10). -- ### See Also * [OMB Circular A-123](https://www.whitehouse.gov/sites/whitehouse.gov/files/omb/memoranda/2016/m-16-17.pdf), Management’s Responsibility for Enterprise Risk Management and Internal Control at § 7(A) (July 15, 2016). --- ## Ensure Privacy is Addressed Throughout the Life Cycle of Each Information System * Agencies shall ensure that privacy is addressed throughout the life cycle of each agency information system. * Citation: Main Body §§ 4(g), 5(a)(1)(c)(i), 5(b)(4); Appendix I § 4(b)(2). --- ## Incorporate Privacy Requirements into Enterprise Architecture * Agencies shall incorporate Federal privacy requirements into the agency’s enterprise architecture to ensure that risk is addressed and information systems achieve the necessary levels of trustworthiness, protection, and resilience. * Citation: Appendix I § 4(b)(5). --- ## Comply with the Privacy Act * Agencies shall comply with the requirements of the Privacy Act and ensure that Privacy Act system of records notices are published, revised, and rescinded, as required. * Citation: Main Body § 5(f)(1)(g). -- ### See Also * [OMB Circular A-108](https://www.whitehouse.gov/sites/whitehouse.gov/files/omb/circulars/A108/omb_circular_a-108.pdf), Federal Agency Responsibilities for Review, Reporting, and Publication under the Privacy Act (Dec. 2016). --- ## Conduct Privacy Impact Assessments * Agencies shall conduct privacy impact assessments in accordance with the E-Government Act and make the privacy impact assessments available to the public in accordance with OMB policy. * Citation: Main Body § 5(f)(1)(i). -- ### See Also * [OMB Memorandum M-10-23](https://www.whitehouse.gov/sites/whitehouse.gov/files/omb/memoranda/2010/m10-23.pdf), Guidance for Agency Use of Third-Party Websites and Applications (June 25, 2010) * [OMB Memorandum M-03-22](https://www.whitehouse.gov/sites/whitehouse.gov/files/omb/memoranda/2003/m03_22.pdf), OMB Guidance for Implementing the Privacy Provisions of the E-Government Act of 2002 (Sept. 26, 2003). --- ## Balance the Need for Information Collection with the Privacy Risks * Agencies shall ensure that the design of information collections is consistent with the intended use of the information, and the need for new information is balanced against any privacy risks. * Citation: Main Body § 4(i). --- ## Comply with Requirements for Disclosure and Dissemination * Agencies shall comply with all applicable privacy statutes and policies governing the disclosure or dissemination of information and comply with any other valid access, use, and dissemination restrictions. * Citation: Main Body § 5(e)(1)(b)-(d), 5(e)(7)(h). --- ## Maintain and Post Privacy Policies on Websites, Mobile Applications, and Other Digital Services * Agencies shall maintain and post privacy policies on all agency websites, mobile applications, and other digital services, in accordance with the E-Government Act and OMB policy. * Citation: Main Body § 5(f)(1)(j). -- ### See Also * [OMB Memorandum M-17-06](https://www.whitehouse.gov/sites/whitehouse.gov/files/omb/memoranda/2017/m-17-06.pdf), Policies for Federal Agency Public Websites and Digital Services (Nov. 8, 2016); * [O]MB Memorandum M-10-23](https://www.whitehouse.gov/sites/whitehouse.gov/files/omb/memoranda/2010/m10-23.pdf), Guidance for Agency Use of Third-Party Websites and Applications (June 25, 2010); * [OMB Memorandum M-10-22](https://www.whitehouse.gov/sites/whitehouse.gov/files/omb/memoranda/2010/m10-22.pdf), Guidance for Online Use of Web Measurement and Customization Technologies (June 25, 2010); * [OMB Memorandum M-03-22](https://www.whitehouse.gov/sites/whitehouse.gov/files/omb/memoranda/2003/m03_22.pdf), OMB Guidance for Implementing the Privacy Provisions of the E-Government Act of 2002 (Sept. 26, 2002); * [OMB Memorandum M-99-18](https://www.whitehouse.gov/sites/whitehouse.gov/files/omb/memoranda/1999/m99_18.pdf), Guidance and Model Language for Federal Web Site Privacy Policies (June 1, 1999). --- ## Provide Performance Metrics and Reports * Agencies shall provide performance metrics information and reports in accordance with processes established by OMB and DHS pursuant to FISMA. * Citation: Appendix I § 4(1). -- ### See Also * [OMB Memorandum M-19-02](https://www.whitehouse.gov/wp-content/uploads/2018/10/M-19-02.pdf), Fiscal Year 2018-2019 Guidance on Federal Information Security and Privacy Management Requirements (Oct. 2018); * [Fiscal Year 2018 Senior Agency Official for Privacy Federal Information Security Modernization Act of 2014 Reporting Metrics](https://www.dhs.gov/sites/default/files/publications/FY%202018%20SAOP%20FISMA%20Metrics-508c.pdf) (Aug. 2018).
